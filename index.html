Abstract

Timbre transfer allows for musicians to control the generation of another instrument by using an instrument they are already familiar with. Maximizing the creative potential of this kind of control requires maximizing the level of interaction between the musician and the sound generator. One way to do this is to enable real-time interaction. In this report, we present our progress on Instrumorph a method for real-time timbre transfer. To perform timbre transfer, We use RAVE (a Real-time Audio Variational AutoEncoder) to compress frames from a single instrument into a latent space. We then experiment with several ways of disentangling the pitch from the instrument's timbre in the latent space, by using filtering operations and matrix decomposition methods. Our end goal is to allow for musicians to `morph' between different instruments in real time, which can aid in the process of creation or performance.
